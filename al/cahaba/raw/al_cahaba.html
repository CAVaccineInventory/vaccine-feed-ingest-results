“POP-UP” TESTING SITES

Cahaba Medical Care has taken testing to the communities it serves and beyond, in the form of "pop-up" drive-thru and walk-up testing sites, in order to ensure that testing is accessible to everyone. Testing is available at no cost to all who are interested, regardless of symptoms or potential exposure to someone with COVID-19. No appointment is necessary, and testing is available for those with or without insurance.

Results are typically received within 3-7 days. We will send each patient an email invitation to set up their online patient portal where they can find their results. Those who test positive will also be contacted by phone, as well as those who do not have an email address or access to the patient portal. 

**Please make sure to bring your driver's license and insurance cards (if applicable) with you to the testing site.

 

Safety for Your Regular Visit

Our top priority is the health and safety of our patients, staff and residents of the communities we serve, which is why we are taking the following precautionary measures to limit the spread of respiratory illnesses, including COVID-19:

At all of our regular Health Center locations, we are screening ALL individuals at entry for fever and signs of a possible respiratory illness. Anyone exhibiting these symptoms will be routed to our nearest Respiratory Virus Evaluation Clinic, where they will be evaluated quickly and efficiently. By taking this precautionary step, we are keeping patients with symptoms of a possible respiratory illness separated from those who are well, which helps minimize the risk of potential exposure to COVID-19 and allows us to continue serving patients who are not currently sick and in need of other essential healthcare services.

Patients coming into our clinics can also opt to return to their vehicles and wait for their appointment after they have checked-in. We will message you when the medical assistant is ready to take you to an exam room.

DENTAL SERVICES

Our dental offices are now open for all procedures and routine cleanings!

We have reviewed safety recommendations from several organizations and would like to inform you of a few new infection control procedures we're implementing at our dental offices to help protect our patients and staff:

  • Our office staff will communicate with you prior to your appointment to ask a few screening questions regarding potential symptoms and/or exposure to any respiratory illnesses. You will be asked the same questions again when you arrive for your appointment, and your temperature will be taken before you enter the building.

  • We will have hand sanitizer available for you to use when you enter the office. You will also find hand sanitizer in the reception area and in other locations throughout the clinic for you to use as needed.

  • Appointments will be managed to allow for social distancing between patients. This may mean that we will have fewer options available to you when scheduling your appointment.

  • We will do our best to allow more time between patients in an effort to reduce waiting times for you, as well as to reduce the number of patients in the reception area.

  • Our staff will be wearing appropriate personal protective equipment and will be cleaning the office well between each patient in order to protect each of you.

For more information and/or to schedule an appointment, please give us a call at the numbers listed below. Our staff is happy too answer any questions you may have about the steps we are taking to keep you as safe as possible during your visit. 

Centreville Dental Clinic: (205) 926-4697

KNOW THE SIGNS

You may notice some of these common signs of anxiety and stress:

  • An increase or decrease in your energy and activity levels

  • Having trouble relaxing or sleeping

  • Crying frequently

  • Inability to feel pleasure or have fun

  • Stomach aches or diarrhea

  • Headaches and other pains

  • Loss of appetite or eating too much

  • Sweating or chills

  • Trouble controlling your worries or feelings of nervousness

  • Having a hard time concentrating

  • Being easily startled

  • Trembling or twitching

  • Being irritable or feeling “on edge”

  • Difficulty communicating or listening

  • Difficulty making decisions
